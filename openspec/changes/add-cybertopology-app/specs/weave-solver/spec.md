# weave-solver — Delta Spec

## ADDED Requirements

### Requirement: Constraint taxonomy — everything drawn is a promise
The Weave solver SHALL accept the following constraints, all authored with the existing gesture vocabulary (no new UX verbs): hand-built quads/patches as **frozen geometry** (vertices and faces immutable, patch boundaries as hard interfaces); color-tagged loops as **flow constraints** (orientation field alignment, position-hard or slide-allowed); guide strokes on bare surface as **soft orientation hints**; pins as hard vertex positions; a pressure-driven density brush as target edge-length overrides; and the active symmetry configuration as a global mirror constraint on both field and extraction.

#### Scenario: Frozen patch survives solve
- **WHEN** the user hand-builds a patch, then Weaves the surrounding region
- **THEN** every vertex and face of the hand-built patch SHALL be bit-identical before and after the solve

#### Scenario: Guide stroke steers flow
- **WHEN** the user draws a guide stroke (no geometry) across an empty region and Weaves it
- **THEN** the solved edge flow SHALL align with the stroke direction in its neighborhood

### Requirement: Prescribed-boundary quadrangulation
Where a solve region borders frozen geometry, the solver SHALL produce quads that land exactly on the existing boundary vertices — matching their count and positions with no T-junctions, no duplicated or orphaned vertices, and no broken edge loops across the interface. Parity or count mismatches SHALL be resolved by placing singularities strictly inside the solve region, never on the interface.

#### Scenario: Seamless interface
- **WHEN** a region bounded by a 37-vertex frozen boundary is Weaved
- **THEN** the result SHALL connect to all 37 vertices one-to-one, edge loops SHALL continue across the interface, and no interface vertex SHALL be a singularity

### Requirement: Regional, incremental solve as the primitive
The solve SHALL operate on a user-selected region (lasso or tap target); whole-mesh solve SHALL be expressed as a maximal region. Regional solves on typical patches SHALL complete in interactive time (~1 s order) with progress feedback, and SHALL be cancellable at any point leaving the document unchanged. Adding or editing a constraint inside a previously solved region SHALL re-solve only that region live.

#### Scenario: Cancel mid-solve
- **WHEN** the user cancels a running solve
- **THEN** the EditMesh SHALL be exactly as it was before the solve started

#### Scenario: Constraint-driven re-solve
- **WHEN** the user draws a new guide stroke through an accepted Weave region
- **THEN** only that region SHALL re-solve around the new constraint, leaving frozen geometry and other regions untouched

### Requirement: Determinism
Given identical inputs (mesh, constraints, region, solver parameters, engine version), the solver SHALL produce bit-identical output across runs and across devices. Determinism SHALL hold for undo/redo replay, golden-file regression tests, and live-link replay.

#### Scenario: Repeat solve
- **WHEN** the same region with the same constraints is solved twice
- **THEN** the two results SHALL be identical vertex-for-vertex and face-for-face

### Requirement: Ghost-geometry accept flow
Solver output SHALL appear as ghost geometry, not committed mesh. Tapping accepts the ghost into the EditMesh; drawing over it or cancelling discards it. Accepted topology SHALL be ordinary EditMesh — every verb and tool (Relax, Tweak, Erase, loop slide, re-Weave) SHALL work on it with no special casing.

#### Scenario: Accept then edit
- **WHEN** the user accepts a Weave ghost and then slides one of its edge loops
- **THEN** the loop SHALL slide exactly as hand-built topology would

### Requirement: Implicit sizing from frozen patches
By default the solver SHALL sample its target edge-length field from the frozen geometry at the region interface, so auto-filled quads match the scale of adjacent hand-built quads without user-set density. The density brush SHALL override this locally; a single global density value SHALL suffice for full-mesh solves with no hand-built geometry.

#### Scenario: Scale matching at the interface
- **WHEN** a region between two frozen patches of similar quad size is Weaved with no density input
- **THEN** the filled quads SHALL grade smoothly between the two interface edge lengths

### Requirement: Assist continuum and ambient ghosts
Weave SHALL be strictly opt-in per invocation: never invoking it yields pure manual retopology. An optional ambient-assist mode SHALL propose next-patch ghosts at open EditMesh boundaries (tap to accept, draw over to override); ambient proposals SHALL be generated by the same deterministic solver and SHALL be disableable. The system SHALL function fully offline with no model downloads and no network calls.

#### Scenario: Assist off
- **WHEN** ambient assist is disabled and the user never taps Weave
- **THEN** no solver-generated geometry SHALL ever appear

#### Scenario: Ambient proposal override
- **WHEN** an ambient ghost patch is showing and the user draws their own quads through it
- **THEN** the ghost SHALL be discarded in favor of the hand-drawn geometry
